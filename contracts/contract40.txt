### Article [X]: Data Protection and Privacy **1. Definitions**  
1.1 "Data Protection Laws" shall mean all applicable laws and regulations relating to the processing of Personal Data and privacy, including, without limitation, the General Data Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679 ("GDPR"), and any applicable national implementations thereof, and all other applicable legislation relating to data protection and privacy.  
1.2 "Personal Data" means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person ("Data Subject"); an identifiable natural person is one who can be identified, directly or indirectly, in particular by reference to an identifier such as a name, identification number, location data, or an online identifier.  
1.3 "Data Controller," "Data Processor," "Processing," and "Sub-processor" shall have the same meanings as in the GDPR. **2. Data Protection Obligations**  
2.1 The Parties agree to comply with all applicable Data Protection Laws in the Processing of Personal Data. Each Party shall implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk associated with the Processing of Personal Data.  
2.2 The Parties shall ensure that all individuals having access to or involved in the Processing of Personal Data are committed to confidentiality or are under an appropriate statutory obligation of confidentiality. **3. Data Processor Obligations**  
3.1 If a Party acts as a Data Processor in the context of this Agreement, it shall:  
   a. Process Personal Data only on documented instructions from the Data Controller, unless required to do so by Union or Member State law.  
   b. Ensure that persons authorized to Process the Personal Data have committed themselves to confidentiality.  
   c. Implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure the security of Personal Data.  
   d. Assist the Data Controller, insofar as possible, in fulfilling its obligations to respond to requests for exercising the Data Subject's rights, as laid down in Chapter III of the GDPR.  
   e. Assist the Data Controller in ensuring compliance with the obligations pursuant to Articles 32 to 36 of the GDPR, taking into account the nature of Processing and the information available. **4. Data Breach Notification**
