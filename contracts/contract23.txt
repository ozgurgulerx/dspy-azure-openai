### Data Protection Clause **1. Definitions** 1.1. "Data Protection Legislation" shall refer to all applicable legislation pertaining to data protection and privacy, including but not limited to the General Data Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679 ("GDPR"), the Data Protection Act 2018, and any successive or supplementary legislation. 1.2. "Personal Data" shall mean any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person ("data subject"). 1.3. "Data Controller" and "Data Processor" shall have the meanings ascribed to them in the GDPR. 1.4. "Processing" means any operation or set of operations which is performed on Personal Data, whether or not by automated means, such as collection, recording, organization, structuring, storage, adaptation or alteration, retrieval, consultation, use, disclosure by transmission, dissemination or otherwise making available, alignment or combination, restriction, erasure or destruction. **2. Compliance with Data Protection Legislation** 2.1. Each Party warrants and undertakes to comply with all applicable Data Protection Legislation in relation to Processing of Personal Data and shall not perform its obligations under this Agreement in such a way as to cause the other Party to breach any of its obligations under the Data Protection Legislation. **3. Data Processing Obligations** 3.1. Each Party shall Process Personal Data to the extent, and in such a manner, as is necessary for the purposes specified in this Agreement and in accordance with the other Party's lawful instructions. 3.2. The Data Processor shall implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk, including but not limited to pseudonymization and encryption of Personal Data, and shall protect Personal Data from unauthorized or unlawful Processing, accidental loss, destruction, or damage. **4. Confidentiality** 4.1. The Data Processor agrees to maintain the Personal Data in strict confidence and shall, as a minimum, adopt procedures equivalent to those it applies to its own confidential data. 4.2. The Data Processor shall ensure that any personnel authorized to Process the Personal Data are subject to confidentiality obligations no less restrictive than those set out herein. **5. Data Breach Notification** 5.1. In the event of a Personal Data breach, the Data Processor shall promptly, and in any case within 48 hours of becoming aware of the breach, notify the Data Controller. 5.2. Such notification shall include full and meaningful details relating to the nature of the breach, the categories and approximate number of data subjects and Personal Data records concerned, and proposed mitigating actions. **6. Data Subject Rights** 6.1. Each Party shall assist the other, insofar as practicable, in responding to any request from a data subject and in ensuring compliance with their respective obligations under the Data Protection Legislation, with respect to rights of data subjects. 6.2. The Data Processor shall promptly notify the Data Controller of any request received directly from a data subject. **7. Sub-Processing** 7.1. The Data Processor shall not engage another processor without the prior specific or general written authorization of the Data Controller.  7.2. Where the Data Processor appoints a sub-processor, it shall do so by way of a binding contract imposing on the sub-processor the same data protection obligations as set out in this Agreement. **8. Data Retention and Destruction** 8.1. Personal Data shall not be retained for periods longer than is necessary for the purpose for which it is processed and shall be securely deleted or returned to the Data Controller upon termination of this Agreement or at the Data Controller’s request. 8.2. The Data Processor shall provide a certificate of destruction upon request by the Data Controller, ensuring complete and irreversible deletion. **9. Audit and Inspection** 9.1. The Data Processor agrees to make available to the Data Controller all information necessary to demonstrate compliance with these data protection obligations and shall allow for and contribute to audits, including inspections, conducted by the Data Controller or an auditor mandated by the Data Controller. 9.2. Such audits shall be subject to providing a minimum of 30 days' notice except in situations where there has been a Personal Data breach. **10. Indemnity** 10.1. Each Party (the "Indemnifying Party") shall indemnify the other Party and keep the other Party indemnified against all costs, claims, damages or expenses incurred by the other Party or for which the other Party may become liable due to any failure by the Indemnifying Party or its employees or agents to comply with any of its obligations under this Clause. **11. Data Transfer** 11.1. The Data Processor shall not transfer or authorize the transfer of Personal Data outside of the European Economic Area (EEA) without the prior written consent of the Data Controller. 11.2. Such transfer shall only occur if specific conditions detailed in chapter V of the GDPR regarding the transfer of data are complied with. **12. Amendments** 12.1. This Data Protection Clause may be revised by the mutual written agreement of both Parties to reflect changes in Data Protection Legislation, regulatory guidance, or business practices. **Section VIII: General Terms and Conditions** **1. No amendment or modification of this Agreement shall be valid unless it is in writing and executed by duly authorized representatives of both Parties. **2. Severability** In the event any provision of this Agreement is found to be invalid, illegal, or unenforceable in any jurisdiction, such invalidity, illegality, or unenforceability shall not affect any other provision of this Agreement or invalidate or render unenforceable such provision in any other jurisdiction. Force Majeure** Neither Party shall be liable for any failure or delay in the performance of its obligations under this Agreement arising out of or caused by, directly or indirectly, forces beyond its control, including, without limitation, acts of God, acts of governmental authorities, strikes or labor disputes, wars, terrorism, civil disturbances, fires, floods, earthquakes, epidemics, pandemics, or other natural disasters, provided that the Party affected shall promptly notify the other Party of the event. **5. Confidentiality** 5.1. Subject to any limitations imposed by applicable law, each Party agrees to keep confidential and not disclose to any third party any non-public information or data provided by the other Party pursuant to this Agreement ("Confidential Information"). Each Party may only use the Confidential Information for the purposes of fulfilling its obligations under this Agreement. 5.2. The confidentiality obligations contained herein shall not apply to any information that is: (i) already known to the receiving Party, as evidenced by written records; (ii) becomes publicly available through no fault of the receiving Party; (iii) obtained by the receiving Party from a third party without breach of any obligation of confidentiality; or (iv) independently developed by the receiving Party without reference or access to the disclosing Party’s Confidential Information. **6.
