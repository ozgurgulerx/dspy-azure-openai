**Confidentiality Agreement Clause** **1. Definitions** 1.1. For the purposes of this Agreement, “Confidential Information” shall mean and include, but not be limited to, any and all technical, financial, strategic, business, or proprietary information, data, or know-how, whether in written, verbal, electronic, or any other form, disclosed by one Party (the “Disclosing Party”) to the other Party (the “Receiving Party”), directly or indirectly. Confidential Information shall encompass, without limitation, business plans, customer lists, trade secrets, market analyses, product designs, project reports, and any other data designated as confidential or that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential under the context of disclosure. 1.2. “Proprietary Data” shall exclusively refer to data unique to the business operations, products, or services of the Disclosing Party, whose confidentiality provides the said Party with a competitive advantage in its respective industry. **2. Obligations of Confidentiality** 2.1. The Receiving Party hereby agrees to hold and maintain the Confidential Information in strict confidence and in a manner no less protective than the manner in which it protects its own confidential information of like importance, but in any event utilizing no less than a reasonable degree of care. 2.2. The Receiving Party shall not, without prior written consent from the Disclosing Party, use, distribute, disclose, or otherwise disseminate the Confidential Information for any purpose other than as necessary for performing obligations under this Agreement. 2.3. The Receiving Party shall restrict disclosure of Confidential Information to its employees, agents, or third-party contractors who have a legitimate need to know for the pursuance of obligations under this Agreement and who are bound by confidentiality obligations no less stringent than those set forth herein. **3. Exclusions from Confidential Information** 3.1. Confidential Information shall not include any information that: (a) Is or becomes publicly known through no wrongful act of the Receiving Party; (b) Was lawfully in the possession of the Receiving Party prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party, as evidenced by written records; (c) Is lawfully obtained by the Receiving Party from a third party who rightfully possesses the information and who is not under an obligation of confidentiality; (d) Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without any use of or reference to the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information. **4. Compelled Disclosure** 4.1. In the event that the Receiving Party is requested or required by law, regulation, or court order to disclose any Confidential Information, the Receiving Party shall provide the Disclosing Party with prompt written notice of such request or requirement, to the extent legally permissible, to enable the Disclosing Party to seek an appropriate protective order or other remedies. 4.2. The Receiving Party shall cooperate fully with the Disclosing Party in attempting to obtain such protective order or other remedies, provided that such cooperation does not require unreasonable expense or potential liability. In the absence of such protective order, the Receiving Party may disclose only that portion of the Confidential Information that it is legally obligated to disclose. **5. Return or Destruction of Confidential Information** 5.1. Upon termination of this Agreement for any reason, or upon the written request of the Disclosing Party, the Receiving Party shall promptly return all physical materials containing or reflecting Confidential Information and permanently delete all electronic forms of such information. 5.2. Any copies, summaries, or extracts of the Confidential Information created by the Receiving Party shall also be destroyed, and such destruction shall be confirmed in writing to the Disclosing Party by an authorized representative of the Receiving Party. **6. Breach and Remedies** 6.1. The Receiving Party acknowledges and agrees that unauthorized disclosure or use of the Confidential Information would cause irreparable harm to the Disclosing Party, for which monetary damages may be inadequate. 6.2. Accordingly, the Disclosing Party shall be entitled to seek injunctive relief, specific performance, or any other equitable remedy from a court of competent jurisdiction to prevent any actual or threatened breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party, in addition to any other rights and remedies available at law or in equity. **7. Term of Confidentiality** 7.1. The obligations of confidentiality set forth in this Agreement shall commence upon the Effective Date and shall continue in perpetuity unless and until the Confidential Information falls within one of the categories listed under Section 3.1. **8. Miscellaneous** 8.1. This Confidentiality Agreement constitutes the entire understanding between the Parties and supersedes all prior communications, agreements, and understandings, whether oral or written, related to the subject matter hereof. 8.2. No amendment or modification of this Agreement shall be effective unless executed in writing and signed by duly authorized representatives of both Parties. 8.3. The failure to enforce any provision of this Agreement shall not constitute a waiver thereof or of any other provision hereof.
