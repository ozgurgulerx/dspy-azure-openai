**Data Protection Clause** **1. Purpose and Scope** 1.1 This Data Protection Clause outlines the obligations and responsibilities of the Parties concerning the collection, processing, storage, and transmission of personal data. This clause is intended to ensure compliance with applicable data protection laws, including but not limited to the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), the UK Data Protection Act 2018, and any other relevant legislation. 1.2 All references to "personal data" within this Clause shall mean any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person as defined under the applicable data protection laws. **2. Data Controller and Data Processor Responsibilities** 2.1 The Parties acknowledge that they may act as either a Data Controller or Data Processor, depending on the nature of the services provided under this Agreement. 2.2 The Data Controller shall determine the purposes and means of processing personal data, while the Data Processor shall process personal data on behalf of the Data Controller in compliance with its instructions and applicable laws. 2.3 Each Party agrees to maintain records of processing activities and shall make these records available to regulatory authorities upon request. **3. Lawful Basis for Processing** 3.1 The Data Controller warrants that it has obtained all necessary consents and legal bases required for the processing of personal data as stipulated under applicable data protection laws. 3.2 The Data Processor agrees to process personal data solely on the documented instructions of the Data Controller and for no other purposes unless legally required to act otherwise. **4. Data Security Measures** 4.1 Each Party shall implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk, including inter alia as appropriate:    a. Pseudonymization and encryption of personal data;    b. The ability to ensure the ongoing confidentiality, integrity, availability, and resilience of processing systems and services;    c. The ability to restore the availability and access to personal data in a timely manner in the event of a physical or technical incident;    d. A process for regularly testing, assessing, and evaluating the effectiveness of technical and organizational measures for ensuring the security of processing. **5. Data Breach Notification** 5.1 In the event of a personal data breach, the Data Processor shall notify the Data Controller without undue delay, and in any event, within forty-eight (48) hours of becoming aware of the breach. 5.2 The notification shall include, at a minimum:    a. The nature of the personal data breach, including the categories and approximate number of data subjects and personal data records concerned;    b. The likely consequences of the personal data breach;    c. Measures taken or proposed to be taken by the Data Processor to address the personal data breach, including measures to mitigate its possible adverse effects. **6. Sub-Processing** 6.1 The Data Processor shall not engage another processor without prior specific or general written authorization of the Data Controller. 6.2 Where the Data Processor engages another processor, a written agreement shall be executed mandating the processor to comply with the same data protection obligations as set forth in this Data Protection Clause. **7. Data Subject Rights** 7.1 The Parties shall ensure that data subjects can effectively exercise their rights under applicable data protection laws, including rights of access, rectification, erasure, restriction of processing, data portability, and objection. 7.2 The Data Processor shall promptly notify the Data Controller upon receipt of any request from a data subject and shall not respond to the request except as instructed by the Data Controller or as required by applicable laws. **8. International Data Transfers** 8.1 The Data Processor shall not transfer personal data to a country or territory outside the European Economic Area (EEA) or an adequate jurisdiction without the prior written consent of the Data Controller and in compliance with applicable data protection laws. 8.2 Where such international data transfers occur, appropriate safeguards must be implemented, such as Standard Contractual Clauses or Binding Corporate Rules. **9. Data Retention and Deletion** 9.1 The Data Processor shall not retain personal data longer than is necessary for the specific purposes for which the personal data is processed, as instructed by the Data Controller. 9.2 Upon termination or expiry of this Agreement, or upon written request by the Data Controller, the Data Processor shall securely delete or return all personal data, unless otherwise required by applicable laws. **10. Indemnity** 10.1 Each Party agrees to indemnify and hold harmless the other Party and its respective officers, directors, employees, and agents, against all claims, actions, third-party claims, damages, and expenses arising from any breach of this Data Protection Clause. **11.
