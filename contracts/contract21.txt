**Data Protection Clause** **1. Definitions**  
1.1. "Data Protection Legislation" refers to all applicable laws and regulations pertaining to the processing of personal data and privacy, including but not limited to the General Data Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679 ("GDPR"), any applicable national laws, regulations, or secondary legislation, as amended or updated from time to time, in any member state, and any successor legislation to the GDPR or Data Protection Act.  
1.2. "Personal Data" means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person as defined in Article 4 of the GDPR.  
1.3. "Processing" shall have the same meaning as defined in Article 4 of the GDPR, which includes but is not limited to collection, recording, organization, structuring, storage, adaptation, or alteration.  
1.4. "Data Subject" refers to an identified or identifiable natural person to whom the Personal Data pertains. **2. Data Controller and Data Processor**  
2.1. The Parties shall acknowledge and agree on their respective roles as Data Controllers, Data Processors, or Joint Controllers concerning the Processing of Personal Data, as defined by relevant legislation.  
2.2. Each Party shall comply with its respective obligations under the Data Protection Legislation and shall not perform its obligations under this Agreement in such a manner as to cause the other Party to breach any of its Data Protection Legislation obligations. **3. Legal Basis for Data Processing**  
3.1. Each Party represents and warrants that it has a legal basis for the Processing of Personal Data as required by the Data Protection Legislation, including but not limited to obtaining necessary consents from Data Subjects or fulfilling contractual obligations. **4. Processing Obligations**  
4.1. The Parties agree to limit the Processing of Personal Data to that which is necessary for the performance of this Agreement.  
4.2. The Parties shall ensure that appropriate technical and organizational measures are in place to protect Personal Data against unauthorized or unlawful Processing, accidental loss, destruction, or damage. **5. Data Subject Rights and Requests**  
5.1. Each Party shall assist the other Party, to the extent reasonably possible, in responding to requests from Data Subjects exercising their rights under Data Protection Legislation, including rights of access, rectification, erasure, restriction, data portability, objection, and rights related to automated decision-making. **6. Data Breach Notifications**  
6.1. Each Party shall notify the other Party without undue delay upon becoming aware of any Personal Data Breach, providing sufficient information to allow the other Party to comply with its obligations under the Data Protection Legislation.  
6.2. The Parties shall cooperate in handling the response to such Personal Data Breach, including any communication to Data Subjects or regulators as required by Data Protection Legislation. **7. Subprocessors**  
7.1. A Party (the "Engaging Party") may engage Subprocessors to Process Personal Data, provided that the Engaging Party enters into a legally binding contract with the Subprocessor that places equivalent data protection obligations as set out in this Agreement.  
7.2. The Engaging Party shall remain fully liable for any acts or omissions of any Subprocessor. **8. Cross-Border Data Transfers**  
8.1. Personal Data may only be transferred to a third country or an international organization where there are appropriate safeguards in place in compliance with the Data Protection Legislation.  
8.2. The Parties shall ensure adequate protection for any cross-border data transfers and enter into further agreements or amendments as necessary for such protection. **9. Records and Audits**  
9.1. Each Party shall maintain complete and accurate records of Personal Data Processing activities to demonstrate compliance with Data Protection Legislation.  
9.2. Upon reasonable notice, a Party shall have the right to audit the other Party's compliance with this Data Protection Clause, subject to the confidentiality obligations herein. **10. Duration and Termination**  
10.1. The provisions of this Data Protection Clause shall survive termination or expiration of this Agreement for so long as Personal Data is processed by either Party.  
10.2. Upon termination of the Agreement, each Party shall, at the choice of the other Party, return or securely destroy all Personal Data, except where retention is required under applicable law. **11. Confidentiality**  
11.1. Each Party agrees to maintain the confidentiality of Personal Data and not to disclose it to third parties except as necessary for the execution of this Agreement or as required by law.  
11.2. The obligations of confidentiality shall survive the termination of this Agreement.
