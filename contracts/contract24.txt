### Data Protection Clause **1. Definitions** 1.1. "Data Subject" shall mean an identified or identifiable natural person to whom the Personal Data pertains. 1.2. "Personal Data" shall mean any information relating to a Data Subject, including but not limited to names, addresses, identification numbers, location data, or any other identifiers. 1.3. "Sensitive Data" shall mean categories of Personal Data revealing racial or ethnic origin, political opinions, religious or philosophical beliefs, trade union membership, genetic data, biometric data for the purpose of uniquely identifying a natural person, data concerning health, or data concerning a natural person's sex life or sexual orientation. 1.4. "Processing" shall mean any operation or set of operations performed on Personal Data, whether or not by automated means, including but not limited to collection, recording, organization, structuring, storage, adaptation or alteration, retrieval, consultation, use, disclosure by transmission, dissemination, or otherwise making available, alignment or combination, restriction, erasure, or destruction. 1.5. "Controller" and "Processor" shall have the meanings ascribed to them under relevant data protection laws and regulations. **2. Data Protection Obligations** 2.1. The Controller and the Processor shall comply with all applicable data protection legislation concerning the handling, processing, and security of Personal Data and Sensitive Data, including but not limited to the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), where applicable. 2.2. Both Parties undertake to implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk, including as appropriate:    a. The pseudonymisation and encryption of Personal Data both in transit and at rest.
   
   b. The ability to ensure the ongoing confidentiality, integrity, availability, and resilience of Processing systems and services.
   
   c. The ability to restore the availability and access to Personal Data in a timely manner in the event of a physical or technical incident.
   
   d. A process for regularly testing, assessing, and evaluating the effectiveness of technical and organizational measures for ensuring the security of the Processing. **3. Notification and Consent** 3.1. The Controller shall be responsible for ensuring that Data Subjects have been provided with all requisite notifications concerning the Processing of their Personal Data and have given valid and informed consent where required by applicable law. 3.2. The Processor shall Process Personal Data only on documented instructions from the Controller, unless required to do so by applicable law, in which case the Processor shall inform the Controller of that legal requirement before Processing, unless that law prohibits such information on important grounds of public interest. **4. Rights of Data Subjects** 4.1. The Controller shall facilitate the exercise of Data Subject rights as required under applicable data protection laws, including but not limited to:    a. The right to access, rectify, and erase Personal Data.
   
   b. The right to data portability.
   
   c. The right to restrict or object to Processing.
   
   d. The right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority. **5. Breach Notification** 5.1. In the event of a Personal Data breach, the Processor shall notify the Controller without undue delay and, where feasible, within 24 hours after becoming aware of the breach. 5.2. The notification shall, at a minimum, include the nature of the breach, the categories and approximate number of Data Subjects concerned, and the categories and approximate number of Personal Data records concerned. **6. Data Retention and Deletion** 6.1. Personal Data shall be retained only for as long as is necessary for the purposes for which the data are processed, unless otherwise required by applicable law. 6.2. Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement, the Processor shall, at the choice of the Controller, delete or return all Personal Data and copies thereof to the Controller, unless further retention is required by applicable law. **7. Sub-processing** 7.1. The Processor shall not engage another Processor (sub-processor) without prior specific or general written authorization of the Controller. 7.2. Where the Processor engages a sub-processor for carrying out specific Processing activities on behalf of the Controller, the same data protection obligations as set out in this Agreement shall be imposed on that sub-processor by way of a contract or other legal act, in particular providing sufficient guarantees to implement appropriate technical and organizational measures. **8. Audit and Compliance** 8.1. The Processor shall make available to the Controller all information necessary to demonstrate compliance with the obligations laid down in this Clause and allow for and contribute to audits, including inspections, conducted by the Controller or another auditor mandated by the Controller. 8.2. Each Party shall bear its own costs in relation to any audit or inspection effort. **9. Miscellaneous** 9.1. In the event of any conflict between the provisions of this Clause and any other Agreement between the Parties, the provisions of this Clause shall prevail in relation to the subject matter of this Clause. 9.2. This Clause shall survive the termination of this Agreement for so long as the Processor Processes Personal Data on behalf of the Controller. **General Contract Terms and Conditions** **1.
