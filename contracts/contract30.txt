**Data Protection Clause** **1. Introduction and Scope** 1.1 In accordance with applicable data protection laws and regulations, including but not limited to the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), this Data Protection Clause (hereinafter referred to as "Clause") is established to govern the processing, storage, and security of personal and sensitive data. 1.2 This Clause shall apply to all personal data processed by the Parties pursuant to the performance of this Agreement and shall remain in effect for the duration of the Agreement or until such time as the data is duly returned or destroyed in accordance with this Clause. **2. Definitions** 2.1 "Personal Data" shall have the meaning assigned under the GDPR and shall include any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person. 2.2 "Processing" shall mean any operation or set of operations performed on Personal Data, whether or not by automated means, including collection, recording, organization, structuring, storage, adaptation, alteration, retrieval, consultation, use, disclosure by transmission, dissemination or otherwise making available, alignment or combination, restriction, erasure, or destruction. 2.3 "Data Subject" means an identified or identifiable natural person to whom the Personal Data pertains. **3. Data Controller and Data Processor Obligations** 3.1 The Parties acknowledge that they may act either as Data Controllers, Data Processors, or both, depending on the specific processing activities undertaken pursuant to this Agreement. Each Party agrees to comply with its respective obligations under applicable data protection laws. 3.2 The Data Controller warrants and undertakes that Personal Data will be:
   
   a) Collected lawfully, fairly, and in a transparent manner in relation to the Data Subject;
   
   b) Collected for specified, explicit, and legitimate purposes and not further processed in a manner incompatible with those purposes;
   
   c) Accurate and, where necessary, kept up to date; and
   
   d) Retained only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purpose of processing. 3.3 The Data Processor shall:    a) Process Personal Data only on documented instructions from the Data Controller;
   
   b) Implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk;
   
   c) Assist the Data Controller in ensuring compliance with obligations concerning data subject rights, security of processing, and notification of personal data breaches;
   
   d) Not engage another processor without prior specific or general written authorization of the Data Controller. **4. Confidentiality and Security** 4.1 The Parties shall treat all Personal Data as confidential and shall not disclose Personal Data to any third party without the prior written consent of the Data Subject, except as required by law. 4.2 Both Parties shall implement appropriate technical and organizational security measures to protect Personal Data against unauthorized or unlawful processing and against accidental loss, destruction, damage, alteration, or disclosure. **5. Data Subject Rights** 5.1 The Parties shall ensure that Data Subjects are notified of their rights regarding Personal Data, including but not limited to the rights of access, rectification, erasure, restriction of processing, data portability, and objection. 5.2 The Data Controller shall respond to Data Subject requests to exercise their rights without undue delay and, in any event, within one month of receipt of the request. **6. Data Breach Notification** 6.1 In the event of a Personal Data Breach, the Data Processor shall notify the Data Controller without undue delay after becoming aware of the breach. 6.2 The notification shall include, at a minimum:    a) The nature of the Personal Data Breach;
   
   b) The categories and approximate number of Data Subjects concerned;
   
   c) The categories and approximate number of Personal Data records affected;
   
   d) The likely consequences of the Personal Data Breach; and
   
   e) The measures taken or proposed to address the Personal Data Breach, including, where appropriate, measures to mitigate its possible adverse effects. **7. Data Retention and Disposal** 7.1 Personal Data shall not be retained for longer than is necessary for the purposes for which it is processed. 7.2 Upon the expiration or termination of this Agreement, or when the Personal Data is no longer necessary for the purpose for which it was collected, the Data Processor shall, at the Data Controller's discretion, return or securely destroy all Personal Data. **8. Transfer of Data** 8.1 Personal Data shall not be transferred to a country or territory outside of the European Economic Area (EEA) unless both Parties ensure that the transfer is in compliance with data protection laws. 8.2 Appropriate safeguards, such as Standard Contractual Clauses or Binding Corporate Rules, shall be implemented for any such transfer. **9. Audits and Compliance** 9.1 The Data Controller shall have the right to audit the Data Processor's compliance with this Clause and applicable data protection laws. Such audits shall be conducted during regular business hours and upon reasonable notice to the Data Processor. 9.2 Both Parties agree to make available to each other all information necessary to demonstrate compliance with this Clause. **10. Indemnity** 10.1 Each Party shall indemnify the other against all claims, actions, damages, losses, and expenses arising from its breach of this Clause or applicable data protection laws. **11.
