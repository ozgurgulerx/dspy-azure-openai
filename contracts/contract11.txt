**Section X: Data Protection Clause** **1. Introduction and Scope** 1.1. This Clause pertains to the obligations and responsibilities of the parties concerning the protection, handling, and security of Personal Data and Sensitive Data as defined herein. This Clause shall be construed in accordance with applicable data protection legislation, including but not limited to the General Data Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679 ("GDPR"), the Data Protection Act 2018, and any other applicable laws and regulations governing data protection. 1.2. For the purposes of this Clause, "Personal Data" shall mean any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person as defined under Article 4 of the GDPR, and "Sensitive Data" shall refer to any Personal Data revealing racial or ethnic origin, political opinions, religious or philosophical beliefs, trade union membership, and the processing of genetic data, biometric data for the purpose of uniquely identifying a natural person, data concerning health, or data concerning a natural person's sex life or sexual orientation. **2. Data Processing and Compliance** 2.1. Each party warrants and represents that it shall process Personal Data and Sensitive Data in compliance with all applicable data protection laws. Each party shall implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk. 2.2. Each party acknowledges that it shall, in relation to any Personal Data and Sensitive Data processed in connection with the performance of this Agreement, be considered as an independent Data Controller and shall not process Personal Data jointly unless explicitly agreed otherwise in writing. **3. Data Subject Rights** 3.1. Each party shall ensure that it has implemented appropriate procedures and mechanisms to facilitate the exercise of Data Subject rights under applicable data protection laws, including the rights of access, rectification, erasure, restriction of processing, data portability, and objection. 3.2. Each party shall notify the other without undue delay upon receiving a request from a Data Subject for access to, or rectification, erasure, restriction, or objection of, Personal Data or Sensitive Data relating to them, ensuring full compliance with the obligations under all applicable data protection legislation. **4. Data Breach Notification** 4.1. In the event of a Personal Data Breach (as defined under Article 4 of the GDPR), the affected party shall notify the other party in writing without undue delay and, where feasible, not later than 72 hours after becoming aware of the breach. 4.2. Such notification shall include, at a minimum: the nature of the breach, including categories and approximate number of data subjects and data records affected; the likely consequences of the breach; and any measures taken or proposed to address the breach and mitigate its possible adverse effects. **5. Data Sharing and Confidentiality** 5.1. Each party agrees not to disclose or share any Personal Data or Sensitive Data with third parties without the express written consent of the other party unless required by law or regulation. 5.2. Each party shall ensure that any employees, agents, or contractors who have access to Personal Data and Sensitive Data are subject to appropriate obligations of confidentiality and have received appropriate training regarding data protection standards and practices. **6. Data Retention and Destruction** 6.1. Each party agrees to only retain Personal Data and Sensitive Data for as long as is necessary to fulfill the purposes of this Agreement or as required by law. 6.2. Upon termination of this Agreement, or upon request of the other party, each party shall securely destroy or return all Personal Data and Sensitive Data, and certify that this has been completed in writing to the other party. **7. Cross-Border Data Transfers** 7.1. The parties undertake that they shall not transfer Personal Data or Sensitive Data to a third country outside the European Economic Area (EEA) that does not ensure an adequate level of protection unless appropriate safeguards have been implemented and agreed upon in writing, as stipulated under Chapter V of the GDPR. **8. Indemnity** 8.1. Each party agrees to indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the other party, its officers, directors, employees, agents, and contractors, from and against all claims, losses, liabilities, damages, expenses, penalties, and costs, including legal fees, resulting from any breach of this Data Protection Clause or violation of applicable data protection legislation by the indemnifying party. **9. Amendments and Compliance Monitoring** 9.1. The parties agree to regularly review and, where necessary, update this Data Protection Clause in light of any changes in applicable data protection laws, technology, or the agreed business practices. 9.2. Each party shall have the right to periodically audit the other party’s compliance with this Clause, upon reasonable notice and during normal business hours, ensuring adherence to the data protection standards as set forth herein. ### General Contract Terms and Conditions #### 1.
