# Force Majeure Clause **Section X: Force Majeure** **X.1 Definition of Force Majeure Events** For purposes of this Agreement, "Force Majeure Event" shall mean any event or circumstance, whether or not foreseeable, that arises after the Effective Date of this Agreement and is beyond the reasonable control of the Party affected, which causes a delay or failure by such Party to perform any of its obligations under this Agreement, including but not limited to, acts of God, natural disasters (including fires, floods, earthquakes), acts of war, terrorism, civil disturbances, governmental actions or orders, embargoes, pandemics, strikes, labor disputes, or any other event of a similar nature. **X.2 Notification Requirement** The Party invoking this Force Majeure Clause (the "Affected Party") must, as a condition precedent to the suspension of its obligations under this Agreement, notify the other Party (the "Non-Affected Party") in writing as soon as practicable, but in no event later than five (5) business days following the occurrence of the Force Majeure Event. Such notice shall specify the nature and anticipated duration of the Force Majeure Event, the obligations affected, and the measures the Affected Party proposes to take to mitigate the effects of the Force Majeure Event. **X.3 Effect of Force Majeure Event** X.3.1 Suspension of Obligations: Upon giving notice as required under Section X.2, the Affected Partyâ€™s obligations under this Agreement shall be deemed temporarily suspended for the duration of the Force Majeure Event, and the Affected Party shall be excused from any liability for the failure to perform such obligations during the continuance of the Force Majeure Event, provided that the Affected Party uses commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects of the Force Majeure Event. X.3.2 Resumption of Performance: The Affected Party shall resume the performance of its obligations under this Agreement as soon as reasonably practicable after the cessation of the Force Majeure Event. The Affected Party shall promptly notify the Non-Affected Party in writing of such cessation. **X.4 Mitigation Obligations** The Affected Party shall use diligent efforts to avoid or remove the Force Majeure Event and to mitigate its consequences. The Affected Party shall keep the Non-Affected Party informed on a continuing basis of the efforts being made to mitigate the effects of the Force Majeure Event. **X.5 Termination Due to Extended Force Majeure** If a Force Majeure Event continues for a period of ninety (90) consecutive days and materially impacts the performance of this Agreement, either Party may, upon giving written notice to the other Party, terminate this Agreement without imposing any liability on the Affected Party for such termination, except that the rights and obligations of the Parties which have accrued prior to the effective date of such termination shall survive. **X.6 Limitations on Force Majeure** A Force Majeure Event shall not relieve either Party of its obligations under this Agreement if:  X.6.1 It was caused by the negligence or willful misconduct of the Affected Party; X.6.2 The Affected Party has failed to comply with its mitigation obligations under Section X.4; or X.6.3 The Affected Party fails to provide the notice required by Section X.2 in the manner and within the timeframe specified. **X.7 Relationship to Other Provisions** This Force Majeure Clause shall not be construed so as to limit or waive the rights of either Party to pursue any remedies available to them at law or equity, except as provided in this Section X. The Parties acknowledge that the rights and obligations under this Force Majeure Clause are in addition to, and not in lieu of, any other rights or obligations under this Agreement. **X.8 Non-Exclusivity of Force Majeure Clause** The invocation of this Force Majeure Clause does not prejudice the right of any Party to terminate this Agreement in accordance with any other express provision herein or to seek any legal or equitable remedies otherwise available under applicable law. **X.9 Good Faith and Fair Dealing** Both Parties shall exercise good faith and fair dealing in the performance of their obligations under this Force Majeure Clause, ensuring that this provision is interpreted and enforced in a manner that reflects the Parties' intentions and the spirit of cooperation underlying this Agreement. ### General Contract Terms and Conditions #### 1.
