**Data Protection Clause** **1. Introduction and Scope** 1.1 This Data Protection Clause ("Clause") is instituted to ensure compliance with all applicable data protection laws and regulations, including but not limited to the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) (EU) 2016/679 and any applicable national implementing laws, regulations, secondary legislation, and any successor legislation. 1.2 This Clause governs the processing of all personal data and sensitive personal data ("Data") transferred between the parties in the course of fulfilling obligations under this Agreement. **2. Data Processor and Controller Responsibilities** 2.1 Each party acknowledges and agrees that, for the purposes of applicable Data Protection Laws, each party may act either as a data controller or a data processor concerning the Data. The specific roles and responsibilities shall be identified within the Agreement. 2.2 As a data controller, each party shall ensure that:    a) It has obtained all necessary consents and provided all necessary notices to Data Subjects for lawful processing;
   
   b) Data is processed fairly, lawfully, and transparently;
   
   c) Data is collected for specified, explicit, and legitimate purposes and not further processed in a manner that is incompatible with those purposes;
   
   d) Data is adequate, relevant, and limited to what is necessary in relation to the purposes for which it is processed;
   
   e) Data is accurate and, where necessary, kept up to date; and
   
   f) Data is kept in a form that permits identification of Data Subjects for no longer than is necessary. **3. Data Security** 3.1 Each party shall implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk, including, as appropriate:    a) The pseudonymisation and encryption of Data;
   
   b) The ability to ensure the ongoing confidentiality, integrity, availability, and resilience of processing systems and services;
   
   c) The ability to restore the availability and access to Data in a timely manner in the event of a physical or technical incident; and
   
   d) A process for regularly testing, assessing, and evaluating the effectiveness of technical and organizational measures for ensuring the security of the processing. **4. Breach Notification** 4.1 In the event of a personal data breach, the data processor shall notify the data controller without undue delay and, where feasible, not later than forty-eight (48) hours after becoming aware of the breach. 4.2 The notification shall at least:    a) Describe the nature of the personal data breach including, where possible, the categories and approximate number of Data Subjects concerned and the categories and approximate number of Data records concerned; 
   
   b) Communicate the name and contact details of the data protection officer or other contact point where more information can be obtained;
   
   c) Describe the likely consequences of the personal data breach; and
   
   d) Describe the measures taken or proposed to be taken by the data controller to address the personal data breach, including, where appropriate, measures to mitigate its possible adverse effects. **5. Data Transfers** 5.1 Transfers of Data to a third country or an international organization shall only take place on the basis of a legally binding and enforceable instrument such as an adequacy decision by the European Commission, a legally binding agreement incorporating standard data protection clauses adopted by the European Commission, or another suitable data transfer mechanism pursuant to applicable Data Protection Laws. 5.2 The data controller must ensure that Data transferred outside the European Economic Area is afforded a level of protection equivalent to that afforded under applicable Data Protection Laws. **6. Data Subject Rights** 6.1 Each party shall ensure that Data Subjects are afforded their rights regarding their personal data, including but not limited to the rights to access, rectification, erasure, restriction of processing, data portability, and objection to processing. 6.2 Each party shall implement mechanisms to facilitate the exercise of these rights in accordance with the applicable Data Protection Laws and any specific procedures detailed within this Agreement. **7. Sub-processing** 7.1 Where a data processor engages a sub-processor for carrying out specific processing activities on behalf of the data controller, the same data protection obligations as set out in this Clause shall be imposed on that sub-processor by way of a contract or other legal act. 7.2 The data processor shall remain fully liable to the data controller for the performance of the sub-processor's obligations. **8. Record-Keeping and Audit** 8.1 Each party shall maintain a record of all categories of processing activities carried out on behalf of each data controller in compliance with Data Protection Laws. 8.2 Upon reasonable notice, each party agrees to make available to the other all necessary information to demonstrate compliance with the obligations laid down in this Clause and shall allow for and contribute to audits, including inspections, conducted by the other party or another auditor mandated by the other party. **9. Indemnity** 9.1 Each party agrees to indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the other party from and against any and all claims, demands, and actions, and all related liabilities, damages, losses, and expenses, including reasonable legal fees, arising out of or related to any breach of this Clause. **10. Term and Termination** 10.1 This Clause shall remain in force for the duration of the Agreement and shall survive its termination to the extent necessary for the processing of any Data relevant to any period prior to termination. 10.2 Upon termination of this Agreement, the data processor shall, at the choice of the data controller, return or delete all personal data, unless applicable Data Protection Laws require storage of the personal data. **11.
