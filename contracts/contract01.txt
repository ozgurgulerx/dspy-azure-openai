### Data Protection Clause #### 1. Introduction and Purpose 1.1 **Purpose**: This Data Protection Clause outlines the obligations of the Parties concerning the Processing of Personal Data pursuant to the General Data Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679 ("GDPR"), the Data Protection Act 2018, and other applicable data protection laws (collectively referred to as "Data Protection Laws"). 1.2 **Scope**: This Clause applies to all Personal Data processed by the Parties in the course of fulfilling their contractual obligations under this Agreement. #### 2. Definitions 2.1 **Personal Data**: "Personal Data" means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person ("Data Subject"). 2.2 **Processing**: "Processing" means any operation or set of operations which is performed on Personal Data, whether or not by automated means. 2.3 **Controller and Processor**: "Controller" means the natural or legal person which, alone or jointly with others, determines the purposes and means of processing Personal Data. "Processor" means a natural or legal person which processes Personal Data on behalf of the Controller. 2.4 **Data Breach**: "Data Breach" means a breach of security leading to the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to, Personal Data. #### 3. Obligations of the Parties 3.1 **Compliance**: Each Party warrants that it will comply with all applicable Data Protection Laws in respect of the Processing of Personal Data under this Agreement. 3.2 **Lawful Basis for Processing**: Each Party shall ensure that it has a lawful basis as required under Data Protection Laws for any Processing of Personal Data. 3.3 **Transparency and Notification**: The Party acting as Controller shall ensure Data Subjects are notified of the nature, purpose, and extent of the Processing as required by Data Protection Laws. 3.4 **Security Measures**: Each Party shall implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk, including, as appropriate:
   (a) Data pseudonymization and encryption;
   (b) Measures to ensure the ongoing confidentiality, integrity, availability, and resilience of Processing systems and services;
   (c) Measures to restore the availability and access to Personal Data in a timely manner in the event of a physical or technical incident;
   (d) Regular testing, assessment, and evaluation of the effectiveness of security measures. 3.5 **Data Minimization and Retention**: The Parties shall ensure that Personal Data is adequate, relevant, and limited to what is necessary in relation to the purposes for which they are processed. Personal Data shall not be retained longer than necessary for the purposes for which it is processed. 3.6 **Access and Correction**: Each Party agrees to assist the other Party in responding to any request from a Data Subject to exercise their rights under Data Protection Laws, including access, rectification, erasure, restriction, data portability, and objection to Processing. #### 4. Subprocessing 4.1 **Subprocessors**: Each Party shall ensure that its subprocessors have entered into agreements on terms which are substantially similar to those set out in this Clause. 4.2 **Approval of Subprocessors**: A Party may engage a subprocessor to process Personal Data only with prior written consent from the other Party. #### 5. International Data Transfers 5.1 **Transfers**: Personal Data shall not be transferred outside the European Economic Area unless the transferor ensures that appropriate safeguards in accordance with Data Protection Laws are in place. 5.2 **Mechanisms**: Such transfers shall be governed by the Standard Contractual Clauses or an alternative recognized compliance mechanism under applicable Data Protection Laws. #### 6. Breach Notification 6.1 **Notification**: In the event of a Data Breach, the Party aware of the breach shall notify the other Party without undue delay and in any event no later than 72 hours after becoming aware of the breach, providing sufficient information for the other Party to meet any obligations to notify regulatory authorities or Data Subjects. 6.2 **Cooperation**: The Parties agree to cooperate fully in addressing the Data Breach, including, but not limited to, undertaking an investigation to determine the nature and impact of the Data Breach and taking action to mitigate its effects. #### 7. Audits and Inspections 7.1 **Inspection Rights**: Upon reasonable written notice, each Party agrees to allow the other Party, or an auditor mandated by the other Party, to conduct audits and inspections to ensure compliance with this Data Protection Clause. 7.2 **Cooperation and Compliance**: Each Party agrees to fully cooperate with audits and inspections and to promptly address any compliance concerns identified. #### 8. Termination and Data Return 8.1 **Return or Destruction of Personal Data**: Upon termination of this Agreement, the Parties shall, at the direction of the Controller, either return all Personal Data and copies thereof or securely destroy such data, unless retention of the Personal Data is required by applicable law. #### 9. Indemnification 9.1 **Indemnity**: Each Party shall indemnify and hold harmless the other Party from and against any claims, losses, liabilities, damages, costs, or expenses incurred as a result of any breach of this Data Protection Clause. #### 10. Miscellaneous 10.1 **Amendments**: No amendment to this Clause shall be effective unless in writing and signed by both Parties.  10.2 **Severability**: If any provision of this Clause is found to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect. **General Contract Terms and Conditions** **1.
