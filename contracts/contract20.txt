### Data Protection Clause **1. Definitions.** 1.1. "Data Controller," "Data Processor," "Data Subject," "Personal Data," "Processing," and "Supervisory Authority" shall have the meanings ascribed to them under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) (EU) 2016/679 or any successor legislation. 1.2. "Confidential Information" refers to all information, whether written, verbal, electronic, or any other form, relating to the business, operations, or financial condition of the disclosing party, including trade secrets and proprietary data. 1.3. "Sensitive Personal Data" refers to data revealing racial or ethnic origin, political opinions, religious or philosophical beliefs, trade union membership, and the processing of genetic data, biometric data for the purpose of uniquely identifying a natural person, data concerning health, or data concerning a natural person's sex life or sexual orientation. **2. Compliance with Data Protection Laws.** 2.1. Each party shall ensure compliance with all applicable data protection laws, including but not limited to the GDPR, and any domestic laws that supplement or replace the same, ensuring they develop, maintain, and adhere to policies and procedures that protect and secure Personal Data. 2.2. The parties shall determine their roles as either Data Controller or Data Processor with respect to the Processing activities under this agreement and shall commit to fulfilling their responsibilities within these roles. **3. Processing of Personal Data.** 3.1. Personal Data shall be processed lawfully, fairly, and in a transparent manner, ensuring adequate, relevant, and limited data to the purposes for which it is processed. 3.2. The Data Processor shall process Personal Data only on documented instructions from the Data Controller, unless required to do so by applicable law to which the Data Processor is subject; in such a case, the Data Processor shall inform the Data Controller of that legal requirement before processing, unless the law prohibits such disclosure. **4. Rights of the Data Subjects.** 4.1. Each party agrees to assist the other in responding to any request from a Data Subject and in ensuring compliance with respective obligations under applicable data protection laws with respect to the rights of the Data Subjects, including access, correction, erasure, and data portability requests. **5. Security Measures.** 5.1. Both parties shall implement and maintain appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk, including, inter alia, as appropriate:    a. The pseudonymization and encryption of Personal Data;    b. The ability to ensure the ongoing confidentiality, integrity, availability, and resilience of processing systems and services;    c. The ability to restore the availability and access to Personal Data in a timely manner in the event of a physical or technical incident;    d. A process for regularly testing, assessing, and evaluating the effectiveness of technical and organizational measures for ensuring the security of the processing. **6. Data Breaches.** 6.1. In the event of a Personal Data Breach, the party experiencing the breach shall promptly and without undue delay inform the other party, and they shall cooperate in taking any measures deemed necessary to mitigate or remedy the effects of the breach. 6.2. The Data Processor shall notify the Data Controller without undue delay after becoming aware of a Personal Data Breach and will provide sufficient details of the breach, the likely consequences, and the measures taken to address it. **7. International Transfers.** 7.1. Personal Data shall not be transferred to a country or territory outside the European Economic Area (EEA) unless such transfer is made in full compliance with applicable data protection laws and ensures an adequate level of protection for the rights and freedoms of Data Subjects in relation to the processing of Personal Data. **8. Sub-processing.** 8.1. The Data Processor shall not engage another processor without prior specific or general written authorization of the Data Controller. In the case of general written authorization, the Data Processor shall inform the Data Controller of any intended changes concerning the addition or replacement of other processors, thereby giving the Data Controller the opportunity to object to such changes. **9. Data Retention and Destruction.** 9.1. Personal Data shall be retained only as long as necessary for the purposes for which it is processed, except as required to comply with legal obligations or to protect the data subjects' vital interests in accordance with applicable law. 9.2. Upon termination of the contract, and in the absence of any legal obligation requiring storage, the Data Processor shall, at the choice of the Data Controller, return or delete all Personal Data processed under this agreement. **10. Audit and Accountability.** 10.1. Each party shall maintain a record of their processing activities under this agreement and shall provide the other party, at their own expense, with reasonable access to records, employees, and facilities as necessary to demonstrate compliance with this Data Protection Clause. **GENERAL TERMS AND CONDITIONS** **1.
