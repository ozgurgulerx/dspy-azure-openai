**Data Protection Clause** **1.0 Purpose and Scope** 1.1 The purpose of this Data Protection Clause is to ensure that all Personal Data processed under this Agreement is handled in compliance with applicable data protection laws and regulations, including but not limited to the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) (Regulation (EU) 2016/679), the Data Protection Act 2018, and any successor legislation, as well as any other relevant local data protection or privacy laws (hereinafter collectively referred to as "Data Protection Laws"). 1.2 This clause applies to the processing of all Personal Data exchanged between the parties in the performance of their respective obligations under this Agreement. **2.0 Definitions** 2.1 "Personal Data" means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person ("Data Subject"). 2.2 "Processing" means any operation or set of operations performed on Personal Data, whether or not by automated means, including collection, recording, organization, structuring, storage, adaptation, alteration, retrieval, consultation, use, disclosure by transmission, dissemination, or otherwise making available, alignment or combination, restriction, erasure, or destruction. 2.3 "Controller" means the party that determines the purposes and means of the processing of Personal Data. 2.4 "Processor" means the party that processes Personal Data on behalf of the Controller. **3.0 Obligations of the Parties** 3.1 Compliance with Data Protection Laws: Both parties shall ensure compliance with all Data Protection Laws at all times during the term of this Agreement. 3.2 Lawful Basis for Processing: Each party warrants that it has a lawful basis for processing the Personal Data exchanged under this Agreement, in accordance with Data Protection Laws. 3.3 Security Measures: Each party shall implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk, including, as appropriate:    a) Pseudonymization and encryption of Personal Data;
   
   b) Ensuring continual confidentiality, integrity, availability, and resilience of processing systems and services;
   
   c) The ability to restore availability and access to Personal Data in a timely manner in the event of a physical or technical incident;
   
   d) A process for regularly testing, assessing, and evaluating the effectiveness of technical and organizational measures for ensuring the security of processing. **4.0 Data Subject Rights** 4.1 Both parties undertake to facilitate the exercise of Data Subjects’ rights in accordance with Data Protection Laws, including, but not limited to, rights of access, rectification, erasure, restriction of processing, data portability, and objection to processing. 4.2 Each party shall notify the other party promptly upon receipt of any request received directly from a Data Subject without responding to that request unless it has been authorized to do so by the other party. **5.0 Data Breach Notification** 5.1 In the event of a Personal Data breach, the party experiencing the breach shall notify the other party without undue delay and, where feasible, not later than 72 hours after becoming aware of it, unless the breach is unlikely to result in a risk to the rights and freedoms of natural persons. 5.2 The notification shall at least:    a) Describe the nature of the breach including, where possible, the categories and approximate number of Data Subjects concerned and the categories and approximate number of Personal Data records concerned;
   
   b) Communicate the name and contact details of the data protection officer or other contact point where more information can be obtained;
   
   c) Describe the likely consequences of the breach;
   
   d) Describe the measures taken or proposed to be taken by the party to address the breach, including, where appropriate, measures to mitigate its possible adverse effects. **6.0 Data Transfers** 6.1 The parties undertake that they shall not transfer any Personal Data outside the European Economic Area unless:    a) The transfer is to a country or territory that the European Commission has determined ensures an adequate level of protection;
   
   b) Appropriate safeguards are in place in accordance with Data Protection Laws;
   
   c) The Data Subject has provided explicit consent to the proposed transfer after being informed of any potential risks. **7.0 Indemnity and Liability** 7.1 Each party agrees to indemnify and keep indemnified and defend at its own expense the other party against all costs, claims, damages, or expenses incurred by the other party or for which the other party may become liable due to any failure by the first party or its employees or agents to comply with any of its obligations under this clause. 7.2 The Processor shall not be liable for any liability arising from any breach of the Data Protection Laws where it is proven that it was not in any way responsible for the event giving rise to the damage. **8.0 Confidentiality** 8.1 All Personal Data exchanged under this Agreement shall be considered Confidential Information, and subject to confidentiality obligations as provided under the applicable confidentiality provisions of this Agreement. **9.0 Term and Termination** 9.1 The obligations set out in this Data Protection Clause shall remain in effect for the term of this Agreement and will survive the expiration or termination of this Agreement for so long as any Personal Data remains in possession of either party. **10.0 Miscellaneous** 10.1 Each party shall appoint a Data Protection Officer if mandated by Data Protection Laws and shall provide the contact details to the other party. 10.2 Each party shall maintain records of processing activities under its responsibility as required by Data Protection Laws.
