**Data Protection Clause** **1. General Data Protection Obligations:**  
The Parties agree and undertake that all relevant activities in connection with processing, storage, transmission, and any other form of data handling shall comply with all applicable data protection laws, including, but not limited to, the General Data Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679 (“GDPR”), the California Consumer Privacy Act of 2018 (“CCPA”), and any other relevant legislation within jurisdictions applicable to the Parties. Each Party agrees to indemnify the other against any loss, damage, or penalty arising from non-compliance with said regulations by the indemnifying Party. **2. Definition of Personal Data:**  
For the purposes of this Agreement, “Personal Data” shall mean any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person (“Data Subject”) that is processed by either Party in connection with this Agreement. This definition shall encompass both electronically stored information and physical records. **3. Data Processing Responsibilities:**  
3.1 **Data Controller:**  
The Party that determines the purposes and means of processing Personal Data is regarded as the Data Controller and shall bear responsibility for ensuring that the collection, usage, and retention of Personal Data are compliant with applicable laws. 3.2 **Data Processor:**  
If either Party acts as a Data Processor, it agrees to process Personal Data strictly on behalf of the Data Controller and in accordance with documented instructions, for the duration and purposes defined herein. The Data Processor shall not engage another processor without prior specific or general written authorization from the Data Controller. **4. Lawful Basis for Processing:**  
Each Party, in its capacity as a Data Controller, warrants that it shall process Personal Data only when a lawful basis under the GDPR or applicable jurisdiction is established, such as with the consent of the Data Subject, the performance of a contract, compliance with a legal obligation, or other legitimate interests. **5. Data Subject Rights and Obligations:**  
Each Party shall ensure that Data Subjects can freely exercise their rights under data protection laws, including the right to access, rectify, erase, restrict processing, object to processing, and data portability. The Parties must communicate and implement procedures to facilitate the exercise of these rights efficiently. **6. Confidentiality and Security Measures:**  
6.1 **Confidentiality:**  
Each Party shall ensure that any person acting under their authority, who has access to Personal Data, does not process such data except on instructions from the Data Controller, unless required to do so by law. This includes maintaining confidentiality agreements and regular training for personnel. 6.2 **Security Measures:**  
The Parties shall implement and maintain appropriate technical and organizational measures to secure Personal Data against accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure, or access. These measures, which shall conform to industry standards, include but are not limited to encryption, access controls, and regular security audits. **7. Data Breach Notification:**  
In the event of a data breach involving Personal Data, the affected Party must promptly notify the other Party without undue delay, and in any event, within seventy-two (72) hours of becoming aware of the breach. The notification shall include, at a minimum, the nature of the breach, the affected data categories, and corrective measures taken. **8. International Data Transfers:**  
No Personal Data shall be transferred outside the European Economic Area (EEA) or relevant jurisdiction unless the receiving party ensures adequate safeguards as required by applicable data protection laws, such as Binding Corporate Rules or Standard Contractual Clauses, and obtains any necessary regulatory approvals. **9. Data Retention and Deletion:**  
Each Party shall only retain Personal Data for as long as it is necessary for the fulfillment of its obligations under this Agreement or to comply with legal obligations. Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement, or upon request by the Data Controller, the Data Processor shall securely erase or return all Personal Data unless required by law to retain it. **10. Audit and Inspection Rights:**  
Each Party agrees to allow the other Party, or an independent auditor mandated by the other Party, to conduct audits and inspections to ensure compliance with this Clause. The audits shall not interfere unreasonably with the audited Party’s business operations. **11. Indemnity and Liability:**  
Each Party agrees to indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the other Party from and against any claims, damages, liabilities, costs, and expenses, including reasonable attorneys’ fees, arising from any breach of this Clause by the indemnifying Party. Liability for breaches of data protection obligations shall be subject to applicable statutory limitations. **12. Miscellaneous:**  
12.1 **Amendments:**  
This Clause may only be amended by mutual written agreement of the Parties and shall consider new legal developments and guidelines issued by competent authorities. 12.2 **Severability:**  
If any provision of this Clause is held to be unenforceable, it shall not affect the validity or enforceability of the remaining provisions, which shall continue in full force and effect. The Parties shall negotiate in good faith a suitable replacement provision that reflects the original intent.
