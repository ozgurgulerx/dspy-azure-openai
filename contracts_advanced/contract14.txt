### Clause 1:###
**Force Majeure Events**  
Neither party shall be liable for any failure or delay in performance of its obligations under this Agreement if such failure or delay is due to any act of God, governmental action, war, civil disturbance, strike, or any other event which is beyond the reasonable control of the parties. The affected party, notwithstanding its diligent efforts, must promptly notify the other party in writing of such an event and its anticipated duration. Performance under this Agreement shall resume as soon as practicable following the cessation of the force majeure event.

### Clause 2:###
**Uncontrollable Circumstances**  
In the event that either party is hindered, delayed, or prevented from performing any of its obligations under this Agreement by reason of events or circumstances that are beyond the control of such party, including but not limited to acts of nature, fires, floods, earthquakes, epidemics, acts of terrorism, labor disputes, or governmental regulations, such party shall be excused from the performance of its obligations during the period of such force majeure event. The party invoking this clause must inform the other party without unreasonable delay and provide reasonable proof of the occurrence of the force majeure event.

### Clause 3:###
**Acts Beyond Control**  
Should either party be unable to perform or be delayed in the performance of its responsibilities under the terms of this contract due to circumstances beyond its control, including but not confined to natural catastrophes, unforeseen governmental orders, cyber-attacks, or other extraordinary influences, such non-performance shall not be considered a breach of this Agreement. The impeded party must exert all reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects of the force majeure and must promptly apprise the opposite party of the situation and the steps being taken in response.

### Clause 4:###
**Extraordinary Events Clause**  
Where a party's performance of obligations herein is rendered impossible by unforeseen events such as natural disasters, acts of government, unusually severe weather, outbreaks of disease, or other forces outside the control and anticipation of the involved parties, said obligations shall be suspended for the duration of the force majeure event. The party experiencing the force majeure must notify the other party at the earliest opportunity and is obligated to resume performance with utmost dispatch once the event is resolved.
